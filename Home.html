What constitutes South Asia?
The region of South Asia is comprised of the southern part of the continent that extends out into the Indian Ocean. This vast area is bordered by four major geographical landforms: the Indian Ocean to the south, the Himalayan Mountain Range to the north, the Arabian Sea and the Thar Desert to the west, and the Bay of Bengal to the East. Just as the landscape of this subcontinent is diverse, so are the countries that make up this region, each with unique customs, languages, religions, and traditions. The eight countries of South Asia are: India, Afghanistan, Bangladesh, the Maldives, Sri Lanka, Pakistan, Nepal, and Bhutan. However, the influence of South Asian culture extends far beyond its geographic region to include the rest of the world due to the growing global diaspora movement.
