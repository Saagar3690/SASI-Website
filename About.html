WHAT WE DO

South Asia Study Initiative (SASI) is a Bay-Area located group that seeks to educate the region about its flourishing South Asian community. This initiative will bring light to this community by documenting immigrant narratives, local cultures, and modes of education. The Initiative means to educate peoples of all descents, including South Asians themselves, about this community which has become an integral part of society in the Bay Area.

OUR VISION
To promote a mutual understanding of the diverse South Asian people and cultures.
OUR MISSION
Encouraging human interactions is key to realizing SASI’s vision. SASI makes this possible through research and community activities, which include mapping the constantly evolving identity of the South Asian diasporas in the greater Bay Area by recording immigrant narratives and facilitating student research. Along with many academic and cultural activities such as book clubs, film viewing and culinary exchange among others, SASI also conducts trips to various South Asian countries guided by specialists in those regions.
SASI does more than just information exchange. This initiative enables participation and discussion to explore the inter-connectedness of South Asian and world cultures.

Dr. Annapurna Devi Pandey
Dr. Pandey's research interests include women’s activism and economic empowerment in India and issues of identity of the Indian Diaspora in the Bay area. Her research is primarily on women’s activism and economic empowerment in the process of globalization in India, particularly in the state of Orissa, India. Also, she have been actively engaged in research on the issues of identity, tradition and culture of the Odiya Diaspora community in the Bay area of Northern California.

Field Study Details
The logistics of the program was very well planned and immaculately executed by the Centurion staff and faculty. Centurion was excited to have us, the UCSC students and faculty on the campus. The excursions were the highlight of our time spent here at Centurion. The students appreciated them to learn about the culture, heritage and traditions of India. They could not have enough of them. Many students expressed the interest to have more excursions in order to have a more enriching experience in India.

UCSC: THEA 80R
Bollywood Dance and Culture in the Indian Diaspora
This course was offered at University of California – Santa Cruz during the Spring 2018 Quarter. Bollywood was discussed through the lens of the Indian diaspora. The class had the honor of welcoming well-established Classical Dancers from the Bay Area such as Snigdha Venkatramani, Jyoti Rout, Anuradha Nag, and Tejaswi Kondapalli to guest lecture about the Indian Classical dance form of their focus. The class had hands-on demos, during when they too could participate and learn some fundamentals of each of these unique dance forms. The class also welcomed student organizations such as Apna Virsa, the UCSC Bhangra team, and Kahaani, the UCSC Bollywood team, to demonstrate to the class how Indian dance forms have manifested in the modern diaspora.
